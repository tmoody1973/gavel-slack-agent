## Post body (ready to paste)

Most of what city government decides is public information. The problem is finding it in time. It's spread across long meetings and PDFs that few people have time to read, so by the time you hear a bar is opening on your corner or a building is coming down, the vote already happened.

So I built a tool to keep track of it.

Gavel is an AI agent that runs inside Slack and follows local city government.

Every day it reads the city's meeting agendas, permits, and property records. When something affects a neighborhood, it posts a short summary in plain English and Spanish, before the vote, with the meeting date and how to submit a comment.

You can also ask it questions the way you'd message a coworker: who owns a property, what a committee discussed, what the zoning allows. It answers from the actual records and links its sources.

It can also draft a public comment and send it to the city on your behalf, with your review, without leaving Slack.

A few practical details:

→ It generates the English and Spanish versions directly, rather than machine-translating one into the other.

→ It doesn't store private messages. It flags when a topic a group has been discussing shows up on an agenda, then stops there.

→ It watches for procedural things that are easy to miss, like an item added to an agenda at the last minute.

I built Gavel as a Slack agent for the Slack Agent Builder Challenge (Agent for Good track). It's an experiment in making local government easier to follow and act on.

If you work in civic tech, local government, or community organizing, I'd be curious what you'd want a tool like this to track.
