### 2. MOO-37 — Validate Legistar content quality ✅
The first "curl-before-commit" gate. Hit the live Milwaukee Legistar API and learned three things, two of which would have silently broken later work:
- **Agenda content is rich** — Plan Commission rezonings/DPDs carry addresses + aldermanic district *in the title*, with staff reports, maps, and affidavits as attachments. The spine is viable.
- **`MatterEXText1-11` are all empty** — Milwaukee doesn't stash structured district/applicant data there. → We extract address/district from the title with Claude, not from fields.
- **`MatterIndexes` are tagged only *after* enactment** (1/25 recent vs 2/2 enacted matters). The 854-term taxonomy is real and good, but useless for *pre-vote* alerts. → **Decision: custom classification** (committee + title keyword + Claude topic-tag against the city vocabulary).
- Cross-checked all of it against the **official Legistar docs** (token policy, 1000-row cap, endpoint shapes) and even found a sharper gotcha: asking for `$top=1001` returns a degenerate ~4-row page, not a clamp.
- Recorded everything as evidence on the Linear issue and propagated the findings into `docs/` + `CLAUDE.md`.

### 4. MOO-38 — Scaffold agent + RTS smoke test ✅ (the #1 risk)

- Tarik ran the interactive parts: `slack run` booted the agent, it **replied to a DM in the sandbox**, and the smoke test returned `ok:true` — then a **real search hit** once a public message was posted. **RTS risk retired.**

## What I learned (Tarik)
- The difference between the **bot token (`xoxb-`)** and the **user token (`xoxp-`)** — RTS needs the user one; the only reliable tell is the prefix.
- `slack run` is a **long-running server** → it belongs in its own Terminal window, not inline.
- Why GitHub's landing page looked "empty": it shows the **default branch (`main`)**, and our work is on a feature branch in the open PR.
- Search has **index lag** — an empty result right after posting isn't a failure.
